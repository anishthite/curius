Four next questions for the follower graph

This page answers the four follow-up questions with the data we have now. The answers are still inspectable: sampled betweenness for bridges, two-hop residuals for follows, Louvain modularity for communities, and saved-link domains as lightweight interest topics.

Who bridges separate islands?

The strongest bridge in the sampled run is Anson Yu with sampled betweenness 0.205. These are people whose neighborhoods sit on many shortest routes through the largest weak component.

For a person v, betweenness counts how often shortest paths between other people pass through v. Brandes gives a faster exact algorithm, but this page uses 256 deterministic BFS sources to keep the build light Brandes 2001.

Which follows look surprising?

The highest-scoring missing follow is Yudhister Joel Kumar → Jacky Zhao. It appears repeatedly in two-hop neighborhoods but is not an observed follow edge.

Common-neighbor scores predict a follow when people share outgoing neighborhoods. A residual asks what the score missed: high-score missing follows are plausible recommendations; low-support existing follows are unusual edges worth reading manually Liben-Nowell and Kleinberg 2007.

Where are coherent communities?

Louvain finds 22 communities inside the largest weak component, with modularity 0.464. The largest groups are mostly separated by follow structure, not by school metadata, because most listed schools are McGill.

Modularity methods search for groups with more internal edges than a random graph with similar degrees would expect. Louvain is a common greedy version; Leiden improves badly connected communities Traag, Waltman, and van Eck 2019.

How do interests travel?

Among 19,240 follow edges where both people have saved-link domains, 86.8% share at least one domain. The graph carries broad reading channels more than narrow topics.

Random walks can mix follows with saved-link topics: start at a person, walk along follows, and occasionally jump to a saved link or highlighted source. Personalized PageRank is one way to keep the walk near a starting person while still letting it explore Haveliwala 2002.
